# C-Shaped Foot Design for ET-QUAD/STARQ

ET-QUAD was created by a team at FSU CISCOR lab for NASA's 2022 Big Ideas Challenge (Lunar Mission). As a team, we created a tri-modal platform that does not require limbs to be swapped in order to transition between modalities. I worked on developing and advising the tri-modal limb design (seen here!) and aided in creating experimental plans and testing procedures. I assisted in the validation and platform testing, and continued to maintain, document, and modify the robot after project completion. 

This directory showcases the C-shaped Foot Design specifically. For a further look at ET-QUAD/STARQ, please navigate up a folder.

## 🤖 The Multi-modal Foot
![ss](https://github.com/AshETQ/ETQ-STARQ/blob/86274272efe328a62c293ad2ae62a76eef522739/C-Shaped%20Foot%20Design/C-foot%20Images/FootIterations.png)

The C-shaped foot design evolved from a conceptual, small scale, 3D printed design that I created for a platform weighing less than 1 kg. As the scope of the project expanded and the NASA team began to design STARQ, the design was iterated on to create a foot capable of running and climbing, with additional fins being added to the leg linkages for swimming. All iterations were characterized through experimentation, with the final design being incorporated and validated on the quadrupedal platform.
